Recommended videos

Below are a few video resources that complement the course material.

  • Veritasium — "What Everyone Gets Wrong About AI and Learning"
    Watch on YouTube — Veritasium
    Description: A short, engaging discussion about misconceptions in how AI and human learning differ. Useful for students interested in the broader context of how computational tools interact with learning and problem solving.
    Suggested audience: All students — watch early in the semester to frame how you use computational tools (e.g., calculators, CAS, and Python) responsibly.
  • FreeCodeCamp.org — "College Calculus - Full Course with Python Code"
    Watch on YouTube — FreeCodeCamp.org (full course)
    Description: A full-length, lecture-style playlist that teaches calculus with Python examples. Good for students who want a slower-paced, worked-example approach and extra practice implementing ideas in code.
    Suggested audience: Students looking for extra practice and those who plan to use Python for assignments or projects.
  • 3Blue1Brown — "Essence of Calculus" (playlist)
    Watch on YouTube — 3Blue1Brown (Essence of Calculus playlist)
    Description: A visual and conceptual series that builds deep intuition for limits, derivatives, and integrals. Excellent for developing geometric understanding beyond symbolic manipulation.
    Suggested audience: Students who want strong conceptual intuition; highly recommended before midterms.
